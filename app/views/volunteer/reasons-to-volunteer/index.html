Benefits of volunteering

We’re really grateful for people who give up their time to help others. We’re here to support you, and make sure your time volunteering is worthwhile and enjoyable.

When you volunteer, you can:

Gain new skills and work experience

Volunteering gives you the opportunity to learn about teamwork, communication, resilience and taking initiative. These are valuable skills you can use in any workplace.

Improve your CV

Volunteering shows your motivation and commitment. This helps your CV stand out when you apply for jobs.

Get a qualification

You can choose to work towards a qualification called ‘Understanding Hearing Loss’, from the National Open College Network (NOCN).

Meet new people

When you volunteer, you’ll meet new people from different backgrounds and feel like part of a supportive community.

Do something you love

You can choose a volunteer opportunity that fits with your interests. Doing things we enjoy helps us feel more fulfilled and motivated.

Try something new

Volunteering is a good way to try something you’ve always wanted. You might like to do something totally different to your regular job, if you have one.

Build your confidence

Doing something useful and productive with your free time is a great confidence booster. Meeting new people and trying new things are good at this too.

Stay active and healthy

Volunteering has a positive effect on our mental health and can help with stress, anxiety and depression. Depending which opportunity you choose, you might also be more active.

Help people in your community

You can help us make a real difference to people in your community by sharing your skills, experience, energy and enthusiasm.

Help us change the future

We’re very ambitious about shaping a better future for the 11million people who are Deaf or have hearing loss in the UK. We couldn’t do it without the support of our volunteers.

The problem

Subtitles are available on most TV shows and films when broadcast on traditional channels, such as BBC 1 or ITV. But not all of these shows and films are subtitled when they’re shown on online video services such as iPlayer, Now TV or Netflix.

Less than 1% of all on-demand content includes British Sign Language (BSL) – either as sign presented content or with BSL translation.

We’ve been campaigning on this issue since 2015. Our campaigners changed the law in 2017, so that the government has the power to regulate online video services and require them to have a minimum level of subtitled content. But the government is yet to put this law into action.

What we want to change

We want the government to act now.

The TV regulator, Ofcom, is currently consulting with broadcasters and other organisations to see how the regulations would work in practice. We are involved in these discussions and are representing the needs of our campaigners.

We’ll then demand that the government brings the regulations into effect as soon as possible – and we’ll need your help.
